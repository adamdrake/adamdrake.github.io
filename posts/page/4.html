    The biggest smallest website

    Introduction I was surfing the web and, as is often the case, I stumbled upon a cool project: the FastestWebsiteEver. It’s “the greatest website to ever fit in a single TCP packet.” I had a think about that for a minute, and checked out the actual site, and noticed that it’s approximately 1130 bytes transferred. Now depending on how fresh you are on your OSI Model, you might remember that there are 7 layers. The data that fits into a single transmissable unit in one layer may be too big for one of the other layers to transmit as a single unit, resulting in fragmentation and reassembly.
